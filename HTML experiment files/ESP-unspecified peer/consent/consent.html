Online Consent Form

 

I am Janet Pierrehumbert, a Professor in the Department of Linguistics at Northwestern University in the United States, and at the Oxford e-Research Centre at the University of Oxford, in the United Kingdom.

 

Together with scientists at the University of Canterbury in New Zealand, I am conducting a research study to understand how people understand words, learn words, and create new words. The project is sponsored by the John Templeton Foundation.

 

If you agree to participate, you will first answer a short series of questions about your age, gender, and language background. Then you will see instructions for playing the word game, and the game will start. The word game has two players, who are put into contact with each other through the game interface. The other player may be a person or a computer program. You will not know who the other player is, but if it is a human, we will tell you before the game starts. While playing the game, you will see pictures or text on the screen and you will be asked to judge, select, or enter words that go with them. You will respond using the keyboard, mouse or trackpad of your computer, and you will also find out how the other player responds. The game will last from 10 to 30 minutes. At the end of the game, you may be asked to provide feedback to the research team by responding to 5 to 10 short questions about your experience in the game.

 

The responses will be analyzed by the research team. They will also be saved in a database that will be shared with other scientists in the future. The database will NOT include any information that could be used to identify you.

 

Your participation in this study does not involve any risk to you beyond that of everyday life.

The game is similar to games that people play for fun.

 

There may be no direct benefit to you by your participation in this research study. Your participation in the study may help us understand how people use and create words.

 

Participation in this study will involve no cost to you. You will be compensated $3 if you follow the instructions and provide complete responses. You will receive this payment through your Amazon Mechanical Turk account. If you do not follow the instructions or do not complete the task, you will not be paid.

 

Your responses to the task will be stored on a secure server under your Amazon Mechanical Turk ID and IP address. No other information about your identity will be stored. Unless required by law, only the study investigators, members of the investigators' staff, representatives of the John Templeton Foundation, the Northwestern University Institutional Review Board, and the University of Canterbury Human Ethics Committee will have authority to see study records that include your AMT ID or IP address. They are required to maintain confidentiality regarding your identity.

 

Data and results of this study may be used for teaching, research, publications, or presentations at scientific meetings. To protect your identity, we will assign a study code number to the record of your responses. We will use this anonymous study code number, rather than your Amazon Mechanical Turk ID or your IP address, whenever data and results from the study are presented or shared with other scientists

 

This study is being hosted by Amazon Mechanical Turk and involves a secure connection. Amazon Mechanical Turk retains a copy of your study data. Terms of Service, addressing confidentiality, may be viewed at https://www.mturk.com/mturk/conditionsofuse. Amazon Mechanical Turk protects the security of your information by using Secure Socket Layer (SSL) software, which encrypts the information. This information is stored encrypted in a database behind multiple firewalls.

 

Your participation in this research study is completely voluntary. You can withdraw at any time by exiting from the web interface for the task.
